Overflowing tanks and faulty ball valves

Water running from an overflow pipe often indicates a worn inlet valve, incorrect float setting, failed washer or a separate system fault feeding the tank. The source tank should be identified before replacing parts because lofts may contain more than one cistern.

Loft access and tank condition

Safe access, boarding, lighting and tank position all affect the job. Old tanks may be brittle, poorly supported or heavily insulated. Photographs of the loft access and tank area are useful, but the condition must be assessed in person before disturbing old components.

Cylinders and immersion heaters

Leaks around a cylinder may come from valves, fittings, immersion bosses or the vessel itself. Electrical testing and connection of immersion heaters requires appropriate electrical competence. The plumbing and electrical parts of the job should be clearly separated.
