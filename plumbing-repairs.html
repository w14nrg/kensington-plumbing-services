Start with the symptoms, not a guessed solution

A good repair begins with what is actually happening: when the leak appears, which outlet has poor pressure, whether a toilet fills continuously, or whether a valve only leaks when moved. Replacing parts without understanding the fault can waste time and leave the original problem unresolved.

General plumbing repairs include

  • Toilet, cistern and flush faults
  • Leaking taps and replacement mixer taps
  • Visible leaks, valves, connectors and accessible pipework
  • Bath, basin and sink wastes and traps
  • Radiator valves and minor heating-side plumbing
  • Cold-water tank ball valves and overflows
  • Shower pumps, hoses, valves and plumbing-side shower faults
  • Washing-machine and dishwasher water connections

Older and altered West London properties

Converted houses, mansion blocks and renovated flats often contain several generations of pipework and fittings. Access may be hidden behind bath panels, vanity units, kitchen cupboards or decorative boxing. The repair plan must take the building and access into account—not just the visible component.

Small jobs and multi-item visits

One small fault is welcome, and several minor items can often be grouped into one visit. Send a numbered list with photographs so likely parts and access can be considered together.
