When a tap can be repaired

Drips, stiffness and poor flow can be caused by washers, ceramic cartridges, aerators, isolation valves or debris. Some branded taps are repairable if the correct cartridge can be identified. Others are more economical to replace, especially where parts are obsolete or the body is badly corroded.

  • Basin tap and monobloc replacement
  • Bath taps and bath shower mixers
  • Stiff or failed ceramic cartridges
  • Loose taps and failed mounting kits
  • Flexible hose and isolation-valve replacement
  • Outside taps and accessible supply alterations

Choosing a replacement tap that fits

A tap must suit the number and spacing of holes, available pressure, connections below the basin or worktop and the physical space around it. Photographs from above and underneath are useful. For bath taps, access may be limited by panels, tiling or the position of the bath against a wall.

Small replacement jobs are welcome

Tap replacement is one of the most requested small plumbing jobs. You do not need to combine it with a bathroom renovation. Send the product link or a photograph of the proposed tap and the existing installation for an initial suitability check.
